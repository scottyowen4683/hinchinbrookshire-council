Hinchinbrook Shire Council — Consolidated MAX Knowledge Base

This document is a structured knowledge base for Hinchinbrook Shire Council. It is designed for use by customer service staff and AI agents, using real Council data where possible and clearly marked guideline service levels (SLAs) that Council can adjust over time.

Important: For any matters involving public safety or disaster events, always follow the latest official advice from emergency services and Hinchinbrook Shire Council’s Disaster Dashboard.

3. Waste and Recycling

3.1 Kerbside Collection – Overview

  • Eligible properties are provided with:
    • One 240L general waste bin (typically red/dark lid); and
    • One 240L recycling bin (typically yellow lid).
  • Bins should be placed on the kerb the night before collection day, facing the road, lid closed, free from obstructions.
  • Public holidays and severe weather may alter collection schedules. Council issues updated advice via its website, social media and local media.

3.2 Kerbside Collection Days (Using 2025 Calendar)

Specific streets and zones are listed on the official kerbside collection calendar and in the “Don’t Waste Hinchinbrook” app. The following summary is indicative:

Collection Day Main Areas / Suburbs (Summary) Notes
Monday Halifax township and nearby localities such as Foresthome, Cordelia and Lucinda. Coastal and lower Herbert River localities.
Tuesday Forrest Beach and surrounding residential areas. Beachside community.
Wednesday Trebonne, Hamleigh and selected rural roads north/west of Ingham. Refer to calendar for exact streets.
Thursday Parts of Ingham urban area. Multiple streets and precincts.
Friday Remaining designated Ingham / district areas. Refer to calendar for coverage.

Residents can confirm their specific bin night by:

  • Checking the current year kerbside collection calendar (print or PDF).
  • Using the “Don’t Waste Hinchinbrook” app with their property address.
  • Contacting Council on (07) 4776 4600.

3.3 Missed Bin Collections (Kerbside)

  • Before lodging a missed bin request, residents should:
    • Check they used the correct day and placed bins out in time.
    • Ensure the bin was not blocked, overweight or contaminated.
    • Check whether neighbouring bins were serviced.
  • If genuinely missed:
    • Contact Council on (07) 4776 4600; or
    • Lodge a request online or via the waste app.

3.3.1 Indicative Missed Bin SLA (Guideline)

Scenario Indicative Response Indicative Resolution Council to Confirm
Genuine missed service (contractor error) Acknowledged within 1–2 business days Return service or alternative arrangement within 2–3 business days Subject to contractor capacity.
Non-compliant bin (late set-out, blocked, overweight) Advice within 2–5 business days Usually no extra pickup unless approved Policy to be confirmed by Council.

3.4 Replacement Bins, Lids, Pins, Wheels and Axles

3.4.1 Ownership and Entitlement

  • Kerbside waste and recycling bins are allocated to the property.
  • Households are generally entitled to one general waste bin and one recycling bin.
  • Residents are responsible for day-to-day care and avoiding damage or misuse.

3.4.2 Replacement Bins

  • Residents can request one free replacement waste bin and/or one free replacement recycling bin per financial year if the bin is damaged.
  • Requests can be made via:
    • Phone: (07) 4776 4600
    • Email: council@hinchinbrook.qld.gov.au
    • “Don’t Waste Hinchinbrook” app
  • Residents should provide:
    • Property address; and
    • Bin number(s) from the side of the existing bin(s), where possible.
  • Old bins are normally collected when replacements are delivered.
  • A fee may apply if a bin must be replaced more than once in the same financial year.

3.4.3 Replacement Lids, Pins, Wheels and Axles

  • Residents can request free replacement lids, pins and wheels for damaged bins.
  • Requests can be made by phone, email or via the app.
  • Damaged lids during wet season can allow water to fill the bin and create an overweight load. Council recommends replacing cracked lids before the wet season.
  • A fee may apply if lids need to be replaced more than twice in a financial year.

3.4.4 Indicative SLA – Bin Replacement and Parts (Guideline)

4. Water and Sewerage

4.1 Overview

  • Council provides water supply and sewerage services to serviced areas.
  • Charges are made up of access charges and, where applicable, usage charges.
  • Water quality and testing information is published regularly.

5. Rates and Charges

5.1 General Information

  • Rates notices are usually issued twice per year.
  • Notices show general rates, utility charges (water, sewerage, waste) and any separate or special charges.
  • Interest may be charged on overdue rates in accordance with Council’s rate setting resolutions and State legislation.

5.2 Payment Methods

  • In person at Council’s Customer Service Centre.
  • BPAY (biller code and reference printed on notice).
  • Online via supported payment services (see website).
  • By mail (cheque or money order) with the payment slip.
  • Direct debit arrangements (subject to Council approval).

5.3 Rate Enquiry SLAs (Guideline)

Request Type Indicative Response Council to Confirm
Balance or simple account enquiry At time of call or visit Identity verification may be required.
Rates search / certificate Within 5–10 business days Fees apply as per Schedule of Fees and Charges.
Hardship application or payment arrangement Acknowledged within 5 business days Decisions may take longer depending on complexity.

6. Pets and Animals

6.1 Registration Overview

  • Dogs over a prescribed age must be registered in accordance with State law and Council Local Laws.
  • New owners and residents must register eligible dogs within required timeframes.
  • Dogs must also be microchipped; Council may request proof of microchipping.

7. Roads, Drainage and Public Works

7.1 Responsibilities

  • Council manages local roads, footpaths and stormwater drainage.
  • State-controlled roads are managed by the Queensland Department of Transport and Main Roads; those conditions are published via QLDTraffic.

7.2 Common Requests

  • Potholes and surface failures on local roads.
  • Blocked drains or culverts.
  • Damaged or missing local road signs.
  • Trees or debris on local roads.

7.3 Indicative Roads SLA (Guideline)

Issue Indicative Response Indicative Resolution Council to Confirm

8. Planning, Building and Plumbing

8.1 Planning

  • Development is regulated by the Planning Act 2016 and the Hinchinbrook Shire Planning Scheme.
  • Applications may be required for:
    • Material change of use (MCU)
    • Reconfiguring a lot (subdivision)
    • Operational works
  • Some building certification services are provided via a fee quote process (Price on Application).

8.2 Building & Plumbing

  • Building certifiers (often private) handle building approvals; Council handles certain plumbing approvals and infrastructure connections.
  • The Fees and Charges schedule includes:
    • Building, plumbing and drainage fees.
    • Building record search fees.
    • Inspection and compliance fees.

8.3 Indicative Planning / Building SLA (Guideline)

Request Type Indicative Response Council to Confirm
General planning enquiry Within 5–10 business days Timeframe varies by complexity.
Information request on lodged DA Within 5–10 business days Formal information requests follow statutory timeframes.
Building record search Within 10 business days (standard) Priority services may attract higher fees.
Plumbing application Acknowledgement within 5 business days Decision timeframe depends on assessment and workload.

9. Community Facilities

9.1 Libraries

Library services are provided at Ingham and Halifax. Core services include lending, access to computers and Wi-Fi, children’s programs, events and local history.

9.2 Hinchinbrook Aquatic Centre

  • Located in Ingham; provides swimming, learn-to-swim, aquatic programs and recreation facilities.
  • Opening hours vary by season; current times are published on Council’s website.
  • Entry fees (adult, child, concession, spectator, season passes) are listed in the Schedule of Fees and Charges.

9.3 Halls, TYTO Precinct and Other Facilities

  • Halls and community venues are available for hire for events and activities.
  • TYTO precinct facilities (e.g. gallery space, meeting rooms) may have hire fees and commission structures for exhibitions.
  • All hire, bond and use fees are set out in the Schedule of Fees and Charges.
